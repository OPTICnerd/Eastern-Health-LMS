Expert Response

 

Major Depressive Disorder (MDD): The New Paradigm

  • Serious, chronic medical illness, with a downward course if untreated
  • Interaction with multiple organ systems
      • Cardiovascular
      • Metabolic
      • Neurological
  • Important to treat both the physical and psychological dimensions of the illness
  • Increased urgency to reach goal of complete symptom remission early in the course of the illness
The Bio psychosocial model remains relevant to our understanding of MDD. The recent paradigm includes understanding MDD as a systemic condition with input from genetic vulnerability, a person’s life experiences and childhood cognitive style, effects of these on their stress hormones and neuroendocrine axis, and subsequent vulnerability and likelihood of developing a MDE. Functional and structural brain imaging and newer technology are helping demonstrate the physiologic effects during an MDE including inflammation.
