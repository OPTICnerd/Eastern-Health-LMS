Overview

The literature suggests that stigma is associated with certain mental disorders more than others.

  • Henderson et al (2013) suggest that psychotic disorders are highly stigmatized often perceived as more violent and unpredictable than other mental health problems.
  • Pescolido et al. (2013) suggest that cross cultural prejudice toward schizophrenia is much higher than depression.
  • Serafini et al. (2011) suggest a genetic explanation for schizophrenia is more frequently associated with stigmatizing views.
  • For health providers Szeto, Luong and Dobson (2013) studied undergraduate psychology students and demonstrated that the label “depression” was perceived more negatively than the general labels of “mental disease, mental disorder, mental health problem, or mental illness”.
  • Ungar and Knaak (2013) suggest physicians label and categorize mental illness as a “functional” Â illness rather than organic which contributes to stigma.

Stigma by association

“Courtesy” stigma or stigma by association is a phenomenon which occurs when people who provide care or services to vulnerable populations are also stigmatized by association with the group receiving care.
