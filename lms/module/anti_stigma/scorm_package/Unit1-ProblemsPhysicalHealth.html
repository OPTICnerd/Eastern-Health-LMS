Under or over reporting of pain
  • The relationship of emotion with pain is complex. Negative emotion that is associated with low to moderate arousal has been shown to enhance the experience of pain and negative emotion with high arousal inhibiting the experience of pain 83.

    Research suggests that individuals with PTSD are more reactive to stress and that this is associated with a reduction in pain perception, which is an opioid-mediated stress analgesia.31,27 A study examining the impact of emotional disclosure of one’s trauma history found that having a history of trauma was associated with increased sensitivity to pain but that disclosing the trauma history was associated with a decreased sensitivity.27

Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE) study

The Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE) study is a watershed in our understanding of the long term health consequences stemming from adverse experiences in childhood.37 This study involved more than 17,000 patients from Kaiser Permanente’s Health Appraisal Clinic in San Diego, California who were asked to complete a simple questionnaire that required participants to indicate “yes” or “no” to whether they experienced certain types of events in childhood, including emotional abuse, physical abuse, sexual abuse, emotional neglect, physical neglect, parents separated or divorced, witnessed domestic violence, someone in the household had problems with drugs or alcohol, someone in the household was mentally ill, or a household member was imprisoned. The ACE score is the total number of these items that are endorsed with possible scores ranging from 0-10.


Prevalence of ACEs

In the ACE study, nearly two/thirds of participants had at least one ACE, about one in two reported two or more ACEs and about one in four reported three or more ACEs.33


ACEs are associated with virtually every major health condition

The ACE study demonstrated a graded relationship between ACE scores and mental illness, health risk behaviours, disease, risky sexual behaviours and disabilities such that the higher the ACE score, the greater the likelihood of each of these.37 Psychiatric conditions include both depression and PTSD.61 Health conditions include autoimmune disease, ischemic heart disease, liver disease, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, lung cancer, and frequent headaches. Health risk behaviours include alcohol and drug abuse, obesity, sexual risk behaviour and smoking. Not surprisingly, ACE scores also have a graded relationship with mortality.37
