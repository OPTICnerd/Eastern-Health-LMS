Introduction

Posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a type of trauma and stressor-related disorder. PTSD can occur after one has gone through an extreme emotional trauma that involved the threat of injury or death to oneself or to others.2

 

PTSD is a disorder that is often severe, chronic, and disabling. It can disrupt one’s social, occupational, psychological, and physical well-being. Many types of traumatic events can result in PTSD and the degree of symptomatology, suffering, and disability can vary widely from one person to the next depending on the nature of the trauma, and a number of risk factors that are associated with development of PTSD. However, PTSD often results in a chronic course.
