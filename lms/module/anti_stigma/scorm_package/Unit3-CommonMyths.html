Common Myths

Common myths about persons with mental health problems/illness

  • Tend to bring it on themselves
  • Don’t try hard enough to get better
  • Get what they deserve
  • Could snap out of it if they wanted to
  • Need to be locked away
  • Are too disabled to ever work
  • Are much more dangerous than the average person
  • Never know what they’ll do
  • Often violent if not treated
  • Commit most of the violent crimes
  • Can’t be relied on

Common myths regarding psychosis

  • It’s a moral weakness
  • His diagnosis is psychosis
  • He’s highly dangerous and unpredictable
  • There’s no hope
  • Patient engagement and behavioural strategies cannot improve adherence, and therefore outcomes, in Psychosis since it is all biological.
