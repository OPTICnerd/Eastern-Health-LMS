Summary

The Cognitive Behavioural and Interpersonal Skills (CBIS) program is an example of some of the supports available to help health care practitioners address the negative effects of stigma.

 

By providing an approach to help implement changed stigmatiaing attitudes, the CBIS program is an example of a program that helps ensure that people with mental health issues are given the attention they deserve in an atmosphere of good communication and partnership where healthy social contact and decreased social distancing is occurring.

 

For more information on the Cognitive Behavioural and Interpersonal Skills (CBIS) program,

please visit the following website: www.gpscbc.ca/psp-learning/adult-mental-health/tools-resources
