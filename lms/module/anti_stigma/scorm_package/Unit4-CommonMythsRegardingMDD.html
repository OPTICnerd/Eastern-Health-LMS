To counter some of the stereotypic myths of moral weakness or laziness it may be helpful for clinician’s to be aware of newer neurobiologic areas of study in MDD. Historically, studying the function of the Brain has been more challenging than other organ systems but new technology is now making study of the physiology of mental illness states more possible.

 

Neurobiologic models for attributing symptoms of MDD

  • Specific areas of brain activity dysregulation in specific areas cause symptoms
  • Associated neurotransmitter dysregulation
  • Structural brain changes e.g. hippocampal volume loss
  • Genetic predisposition
  • Autoimmune dysregulation
  • Inflammatory dysregulation with systemic effects, cardiac, metabolic, and neurologic

Be careful with diagnostic overshadowing and skipping over physical exam and appropriate investigations.

 

Asking about suicide reduces the likelihood of attempts as it is often a relief for people to be able to let someone else know. Most people who have attempted suicide have seen their health provider, often a family physician, within the few months before.
