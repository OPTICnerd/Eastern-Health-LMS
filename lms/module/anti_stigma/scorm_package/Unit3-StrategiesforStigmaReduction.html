Overview

Health Care providers are in a unique and privileged position to reduce stigma. One of the best ways is to provide and model  high quality equitable health care for persons with mental health condition for both their physical and mental health problems.

 

Best evidenced interventions include:

  • contact based education;
  • ensuring equity for mental health by structural policy; and
  • legislative change in keeping with the physician’s role as advocate.

Promising newer research demonstrates that enhancing health care provider medical expert clinical skills can increase their sense of mastery and clinical effectiveness when treating patients and thereby reduce avoidance (Weinerman, R et  al).

 

Increasing organic biologic knowledge of mental health conditions, in addition to the psychosocial aspects, may help  physicians legitimize mental illness and broaden the limited mind vs. body split, physical vs. mental, functional vs. organic paradigm that unknowingly structures physician thinking and behavior. (Ungar T, Knaak  S, 2013).
