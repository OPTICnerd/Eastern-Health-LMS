Chapter Summary

There are many causes of poor health, including biology, personal behaviours and access to health services.

  • Health is also profoundly influenced by social factors, such as income and social status.

  • The CODE RED research carried out in Hamilton illustrates well the effects of social determinants on health. For example, the same population in the lowest 20% for family income has the highest hospital admission rate. Â 

  • The literature offers three reasons that explain the impact of income on health: material deprivation, psychological impact and personal behaviours.

  • Differences in epigenetics may further mediate some of the differences in health outcomes between income groups.

  • Air pollution, smoking and education have also been linked with low income and poor health.
