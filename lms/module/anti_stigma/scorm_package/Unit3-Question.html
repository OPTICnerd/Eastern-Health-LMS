Question

Which of these three strategies for changing and reducing stigma is most effective?

 

1

Contact - challenging public attitudes about mental illness through direct interactions with persons who have the disorders.
2 Education - replacing myths about mental illness with accurate conceptions.
3. Protest - seeking to suppress stigmatizing attitudes about mental illness.
     

Feedback

Contact with persons with lived experience of mental health problems or experience relating to a person living with mental illness, yet living a full life in recovery (Sadow & Ryder, 2008), has been shown to be the most powerful intervention and produced the most positive change in attitudes.

 

Education has been shown to lead to some improved attributions about mental illness.

 

Protest did not yield any significant change in attributions about mental illness (may yield unwanted rebound effect)

 

Corrigan, P. River LP. Lundin RK et al. Three Strategies for Changing Attributions about Severe Mental Illness Schizophrenia Bulletin, 27(2):187-195,2001.

Incorrect. The correct answer is c.
All three factors appeared related and affect residents in the same city areas, where those with low income mostly live.
