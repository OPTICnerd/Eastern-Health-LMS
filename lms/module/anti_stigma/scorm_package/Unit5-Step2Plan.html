Step 2: Plan

  • Problem List
  • Action Plan

The problem list and action plan are tools used by the CBIS program to help your patient become an active partner in their treatment and recovery. These tools encourage the patient to:

  • reflect on all of their problems/stressors and strengths/resources.
  • encourages full patient partnership, healthy social contact and good communication.

At the end of the screening interview, send your patient home with a blank sheet of paper in the manual that asks them to list every single one of their problems from not being able to afford the medications being prescribed to poverty to sleeplessness and isolation. Also, send them home with a strengths/resource list to find out who this suffering person is underneath all the symptoms, to find out what their potential could be and where their recovery should be aimed.

Using the organized approach, don’t be afraid to find out ALL the patient’s problems/stressors and Strengths (personal internal resources). CBIS will help you organize them according to the categories in the manual that could help them manage their problems – Activation, Relaxation, Cognition changes, Lifestyle changes, Medications, referrals to other resources. This takes 2 -15 minute visits or one 30 minute visit. It’s a care plan, and points to you and the patient working together to manage their problems, not you alone fixing them. The patient feels heard and engaged, more respected and less stigmatized.
