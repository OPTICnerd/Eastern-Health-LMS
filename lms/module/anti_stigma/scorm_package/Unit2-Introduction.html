Socioeconomic Status and Health

Here’s some of the evidence reported about low income and individual health.

 

  • Between 1991 and 2001, Canadians who enjoyed a higher socioeconomic status (defined by education, occupation or income) lived longer.

  • On the 1996-97 National Population Health Survey, 73% of Canadians in the highest income group rated their health as excellent, while only 47% in the lowest income group did so.

Socioeconomic Status and Use of Health Services

A review of 14 peer-reviewed studies on utilization of care and income points to the following conclusions:

  • The evidence of an association between lower socioeconomic status (SES) and use of family physicians is relatively equally divided among studies that report increased use, decreased use, and no relationship.

  • A majority of studies find an association between lower SES and increased hospital use.[13]

  • A majority of studies find an association between lower SES and less use of specialist services compared to persons of higher SES.
