Common Presentations of Patients with a Trauma History

Although there is no definitive presentation for patients with a history of traumatic experience and PTSD symptoms, research and clinical experience has identified a number of behaviours and physical complaints with which these patients often present.

 

Behaviours specific to seeking health care

  • Frequent cancellations
  • Non-adherence with treatment recommendations
  • Avoiding physical examinations
  • Avoiding seeking health care
  • Uncomfortable with a specific gender of health care practitioner
  • Frequent use of urgent or emergency care facilities
  • Appearing distracted or unable to focus (possible dissociation)
  • Easily startled

Physical complaints/problems

  • Unexplained gastrointestinal symptoms
  • Unexplained chronic pain
  • Headaches, migraines
  • Pelvic pain
  • Self-injury (cutting, skin picking)
  • Eating disorder
  • Addiction
  • Sleep disturbance
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Chronic fatigue

Emotional/psychological complaints

  • Depressive symptoms
  • Suicidal ideation, parasuicidality
  • Anxiety
  • Dissociation
  • Anger/rage
  • Relationship problems

Please Note: This list is not exhaustive and it is not meant to indicate certainty of a trauma history or diagnosis of PTSD. Instead, these behaviors and complaints should raise the question in the mind of the physician as to whether the patient has a trauma history.
