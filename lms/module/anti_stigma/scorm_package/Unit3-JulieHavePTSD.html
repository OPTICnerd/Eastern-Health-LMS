Possible signs of PTSD in this case include:

  • History of childhood sexual abuse and multiple sexual assaults in adulthood (Crit. A)
    • Exposure to sexual violence
  • Binge drinking (Crit. C, E)
    • Drinking can be a way to avoid distressing memories, thoughts or feelings associated with her trauma and that might be activated when she goes to her local bar
    • Binge drinking while at her local bar can be considered a reckless or self-destructive behaviour because it puts her in a vulnerable position
  • Self injury (cutting) (Crit. C, E)
    • Cutting can be a way to avoid feelings that are linked to her abuse
    • Cutting can be a way to manage the arousal associated with overwhelming distress
  • Self blame, low self esteem (Crit. D)
    • This suggests negative alterations in cognition and likely mood
  • Amnesia for ages 6-12 (Crit. D)
    • Suggests dissociative amnesia to block out memories of childhood abuse
  • Dissociation (possibly a flashback) in response to being asked to lie on the exam table (Crit. B)
    • The dissociative response in reaction to the instruction to lie down suggests that she may have been experiencing a flashback
  • Possible hypervigilance response during the palpation of her stomach (Crit. E)
    • Suddenly calling out “stop” suggests either a hypervigilance or an exaggerated startle response or both.

 

Diagnostic Criteria for PTSD

  1. Exposure to actual or threatened death, serious injury or sexual violence
  2. Presence of at least one intrusion symptom
  3. Persistent avoidance of stimuli associated with the event
  4. Negative cognitions or moods associated with the event
  5. Marked alterations in arousal or reactivity after the event
  6. Duration of the symptoms
  7. Functional significance
  8. Exclusion criteria
