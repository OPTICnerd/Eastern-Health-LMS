Introduction

There are many ways in which physicians can intervene to reduce the impact of poverty on health. Starting with the core family practice relationship - that between an individual health provider and an individual patient or family in a primary care office - the following simple, three-step approach has been developed to addressing poverty.

  1. Screen everyone!

  2. Adjust risk: factor poverty into clinical decision-making like other risk factors.

  3. Intervene: use 7 simple questions to help patients living in poverty.

Poverty: A clinical tool for primary care in Ontario - One of many initiatives taking place across Canada.
