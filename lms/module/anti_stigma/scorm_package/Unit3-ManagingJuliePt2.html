Managing Julie (Part 2)

Julie returns two weeks later and you ask how she has been since you last saw her. Julie states that it’s been a rough two weeks. She states that she’s been having more urges to cut ever since the last medical appointment and that last night she did, in fact, cut herself. Her doctor asks if she has any idea as to why. Julie says “I’m not sure but I think it’s because I told you about the abuse.”

 

Is Julie a good candidate for psychotherapy?

 


 


Chosen Answer: Yes, stage 1 trauma therapy

 

Correct Answer: Yes, stage 1 trauma therapy

This answer might seem counterintuitive because didn’t talking about the abuse exacerbate her symptoms? In fact, Julie is a good candidate for stage 1 trauma therapy because the focus of stage 1 trauma therapy is safety and stabilization. Julie has demonstrated two important things that make it clear that she is appropriate for stage 1 trauma therapy. First, she can see a connection between talking about her trauma history and having urges to cut. The fact that she can see this connection and talk about it is a positive sign about her ability to reflect on herself in relation to her trauma history and suggests she is a good candidate for psychotherapy. Second, it appears that talking about the abuse resulted in her being flooded with unbearable feelings related to the abuse, which led to strong urges to cut in order to manage the pain. It is for this reason that the focus of therapy should be on safety and stabilization. Julie needs to learn better coping strategies for dealing with the intense feelings that are activated when she is triggered so that she can keep herself safe and stable. This is necessary before she can begin the stage 2 work of talking about her trauma memories.

 

Chosen Answer: Yes, stage 2 trauma therapy

Julie can see a connection between talking about her trauma history and having urges to cut and she can talk about it. This is a positive sign about her ability to reflect on herself in relation to her trauma history and suggests she is a good candidate for psychotherapy. However, talking about her trauma history led to her having the urge to cut and to actually cutting herself. This suggests that she might not yet be ready for stage 2 trauma therapy where the focus is on talking about traumatic memories. Stage 2 work involves having survivors revisit their traumatic past so that can integrate those experiences into a revised sense of self and the world. Stage 2 work inevitably stirs up aversive emotional states and the survivor must have the capacity to tolerate those feelings as uncomfortable as they may be. Once Julie shows a capacity to manage difficult feelings without engaging in self-injury and demonstrates that she is reasonably stable in her life, then stage 2 may be an appropriate next step in her healing process.
