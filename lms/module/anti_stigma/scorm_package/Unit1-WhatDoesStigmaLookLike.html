What Does Stigma Look Like?

  • Many people living with a mental illness say the stigma they face is often worse than the illness itself
  • Stigma is one of the major barriers preventing people from seeking help
  • 40% of Canadian parents would not tell anyone if their child had a mental illness
  • Only 1 child in 6 who is diagnosed with a mental health problem gets help
  • One third of the news stories  about mental health focus on murder and violent crimes

Nettleton, S. (2006) The sociology of health and illness. Cambridge UK. Polity Press pp.95 ISBN 10:0-7456-2827-3

 

Disease and Illness Associated Stigma: Canadian Top 3

  1. Mental Health problems (schizophrenia, depression)
  2. Contagious Diseases (SARS or HIV/AIDS)
  3. “Diseases of Excess”-(e.g. obesity, alcoholism or drug addiction)

Keusch GT, Wilentz J, Kleinman A, 2006 Stigma and Global Health: Developing a Research Agenda. Lancet, 367, 525-27

Wailoo K, 2006. Stigma, race and disease in 20th century America. Lancet,367,531-33.
