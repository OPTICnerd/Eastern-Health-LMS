Question 4

What would you say to her at this point?

 

Expert Response

 

Basic information about clinical major depression and treatment can be explained as part of what is called “psychoeducation”. Information materials may be helpful for persons to take away or recommendations to credible information sources. Address common myths and explain it’s not their fault, it’s not a weakness or a sign of intelligence or effort, it’s a real medical condition, and it can get better, provide hope. If needed, discuss accommodations or time off work as with any medical illness. Ask about suicide and document a risk assessment. Discuss with them a safety plan to go to an emergency room or crisis services in your region.Â Finally, provide hope for recovery.
