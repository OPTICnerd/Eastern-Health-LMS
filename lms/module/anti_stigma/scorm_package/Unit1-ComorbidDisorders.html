Comorbid Disorders

Large epidemiological surveys have shown that of those with a lifetime history of PTSD, 88.3% of men and 79% of women will have a lifetime history of at least one other psychiatric diagnosis.55 Patients who present with PTSD often have comorbid diagnoses of:

Research and clinical experience also suggests that patients suffering from PTSD show increased rates of somatic symptom disorder77, dissociative identity disorder49, borderline personality disorders 109, conversion disorders15, and eating disorders 94. Therefore, it is recommended that these conditions also be considered in the differential diagnosis of a patient with PTSD.

Dissociative identity disorder is most often due to childhood abuse and neglect trauma. Dissociative identity disorder (previously known as multiple personality disorder) has a prevalence rate of 1-3% of the population (International Society for the Study of Trauma and Dissociation, 2011).
