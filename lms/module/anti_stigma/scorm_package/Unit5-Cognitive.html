Cognitive Behavioural Interpersonal Skills (CBIS) program

We are going to introduce you to one structured time-efficient program which will demonstrate ways in which you can implement your newly changed attitudes by enhancing healthy social contact and communication, and decreasing social distance with your patients. This program has been evaluated in BC to increase Family Physician comfort and confidence in treating patients with mental health issues, maintaining its results for 6 months at least. As well, this program has been evaluated to improve the patient experience so that they feel more heard and understood, more comfortable and more of a partner with their family physician or healthcare professional. They also felt they had more questions answered and had more materials and activities to use in their treatment. This program is called the Cognitive Behavioural Interpersonal Skills (CBIS) program, was developed in BC, and forms the core of the BC Practice Support Program which has now trained >1400/3300 of BC’s family physicians.

 

We will go through the step by step components of CBIS from diagnostic screening through to the development of an action plan to specific CBT training skills for your patients highlighting how the tools enhance healthy social contact and communication and decrease social distance.

If you are interested in exploring CBIS, visit the free website Practice Support Program.
